Welcome to the Dry Lab

Where computation fuels biology — modeling, simulation, and prediction for ARGUS-2440.

The dry lab is our project’s digital engine. From machine learning models predicting soil ammonia levels, to ODE-based simulations of nitrogen flux, we ensure designs are optimized before entering the wet lab. This saves time, resources, and helps us explore new ideas at scale.

Our Computational Workflow

Modeling

Using differential equations and graph-based tools, we capture the dynamics of nitrate reduction pathways. This provides predictive insight into how engineered microbes behave in complex environments.

Simulation

We run computational simulations to test genetic circuit stability, bioreactor performance, and soil nutrient responses. These predictions reduce trial-and-error in the wet lab.

Machine Learning

Our ML models predict ammonia production under varying soil conditions, integrating experimental and environmental data. This helps optimize strain design and deployment strategies.

Integration

We integrate modeling and experiments in a design-build-test-learn cycle, ensuring computational predictions inform wet lab efforts in real time.
